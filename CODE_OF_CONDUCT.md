# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

<!--
  =============================================================================
  CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md — ZYNC Desktop Application
  =============================================================================

  This Code of Conduct is adapted from the Contributor Covenant, version 2.1,
  available at https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct

  This document establishes the behavioral expectations for all participants
  in the ZYNC project community, including contributors, maintainers, and users
  interacting through GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, and other
  project communication channels.

  The purpose of this Code of Conduct is to ensure that the ZYNC community
  remains a welcoming, inclusive, and harassment-free environment for everyone,
  regardless of their background, identity, or level of experience.

  =============================================================================
-->

## Our Pledge

<!--
  This pledge establishes the foundation of our community's values. By
  participating in the ZYNC project, all community members agree to uphold
  these principles and work together to create a positive environment.
-->

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our
community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body
size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender
identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status,
nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual
identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming,
diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

<!--
  These standards provide concrete examples of behavior that contributes to
  a positive environment and behavior that is considered unacceptable. These
  examples are not exhaustive but serve as guidelines for community members
  to understand the expectations.
-->

### Examples of Behavior That Contributes to a Positive Environment

<!--
  Positive behaviors that all community members should strive to exhibit.
  These behaviors create a welcoming atmosphere and encourage collaboration.
-->

* **Demonstrating empathy and kindness** toward other people
  - Acknowledge that everyone has different levels of experience and expertise
  - Be patient with newcomers who may not be familiar with the project's
    conventions or technical stack
  - Offer constructive help and guidance when someone asks questions

* **Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences**
  - Recognize that there are often multiple valid approaches to solving a
    technical problem, and engage in thoughtful discussion about trade-offs
  - Avoid dismissing ideas without providing constructive alternatives
  - Value diverse perspectives as a strength of the community

* **Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback**
  - Frame feedback in terms of the code or behavior, not the person
  - When receiving feedback, consider it as an opportunity to learn and improve
  - Thank reviewers for their time and effort in providing feedback

* **Accepting responsibility and apologizing to those affected by our mistakes**
  - Everyone makes mistakes; what matters is how we handle them
  - Correct mistakes promptly and learn from the experience
  - Acknowledge the impact of mistakes on others affected by them

* **Focusing on what is best not just for us as individuals, but for the
  overall community**
  - Consider the long-term impact of decisions on the project and community
  - Prioritize the health of the project over individual preferences
  - Share knowledge and help others grow in their abilities

### Examples of Unacceptable Behavior

<!--
  These behaviors are not tolerated in the ZYNC community. Any instances
  of such behavior will be addressed by the project maintainers according
  to the enforcement guidelines outlined below.
-->

* **The use of sexualized language or imagery**, and sexual attention or
  advances of any kind
  - This includes but is not limited to: unwelcome sexual comments, jokes,
    or innuendos in code review, issue discussions, or community channels
  - Sexualized profile images or usernames are also not acceptable

* **Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments**, and personal or political
  attacks
  - Deliberately provoking or antagonizing other community members
  - Making disparaging remarks about someone's technical abilities
  - Engaging in flame wars or unproductive arguments

* **Public or private harassment**
  - Repeatedly contacting someone who has asked you to stop
  - Following someone across multiple platforms or communication channels
  - Intimidating or threatening behavior

* **Publishing others' private information** (physical or email address)
  without their explicit permission (also known as "doxxing")
  - This includes sharing personal information found through public records
    or social media without consent

* **Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate** in a
  professional setting
  - Excessive use of profanity
  - Sustained disruption of community events or discussions
  - Advocating for or encouraging any of the above behaviors

## Enforcement Responsibilities

<!--
  Community leaders are responsible for enforcing this Code of Conduct.
  This section clarifies their role and authority in maintaining community
  standards.
-->

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of
acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in
response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive,
or harmful.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject
comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are
not aligned to this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation
decisions when appropriate.

## Scope

<!--
  This section defines where and when this Code of Conduct applies. It covers
  both official project spaces and public representations of the project.
-->

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, and also applies when
an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces.
Examples of representing our community include:

* Using an official e-mail address (e.g., @zync.io)
* Posting via an official social media account
* Acting as an appointed representative at an online or offline event
* Engaging in project-related activities on GitHub (issues, PRs, discussions)
* Participating in project-related chat channels (Discord, Slack, etc.)

## Enforcement

<!--
  This section provides clear instructions on how to report violations of
  this Code of Conduct. Reports are handled confidentially and taken
  seriously by the project maintainers.
-->

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be
reported to the community leaders responsible for enforcement at:

📧 **conduct@zync.io**

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. All
community leaders are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the
reporter of any incident.

### What to Include in a Report

<!--
  Providing detailed information helps the enforcement team investigate
  and respond to reports effectively.
-->

When reporting an incident, please include:

1. **Your contact information** (so we can follow up if needed)
2. **Names or usernames** of the individuals involved
3. **Description of the behavior** — what happened, and why you consider it
   a violation of this Code of Conduct
4. **Date and time** of the incident
5. **Location** where the incident occurred (e.g., GitHub issue #123,
   Discord channel, email thread)
6. **Any supporting evidence** — screenshots, links, or other documentation
7. **Whether the incident is ongoing** or has concluded

### Response from Maintainers

<!--
  This section sets expectations for how reports will be handled and
  the timeline for responses.
-->

You can expect the following from our enforcement team:

* **Acknowledgment** of your report within 24 hours
* **Assessment** of the situation within 72 hours
* **Decision** on appropriate action within 7 days for non-urgent matters
* **Immediate action** for situations involving active threats or harassment
* **Follow-up** communication about the outcome of your report

## Enforcement Guidelines

<!--
  These guidelines provide a framework for determining consequences.
  They ensure that enforcement is proportional and consistent across
  different types of violations.
-->

Community leaders will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in determining
the consequences for any action they deem in violation of this Code of Conduct:

### 1. Correction

<!--
  Correction is the lowest level of response, used for minor first-time
  violations. It serves as a gentle reminder of community standards.
-->

**Community Impact**: Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed
unprofessional or unwelcome in the community.

**Consequence**: A private, written warning from community leaders, providing
clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the
behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

### 2. Warning

<!--
  A warning is a more serious response, typically used for repeated minor
  violations or a single significant violation.
-->

**Community Impact**: A violation through a single incident or series of
actions.

**Consequence**: A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No
interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with
those enforcing the Code of Conduct, for a specified period of time. This
includes avoiding interactions in community spaces as well as external channels
like social media. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent
ban.

### 3. Temporary Ban

<!--
  A temporary ban is used for serious violations or a pattern of behavior
  that indicates a disregard for community standards.
-->

**Community Impact**: A serious violation of community standards, including
sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence**: A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public
communication with the community for a specified period of time. No public or
private interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction
with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, is allowed during this period.
Violating these terms may lead to a permanent ban.

### 4. Permanent Ban

<!--
  A permanent ban is reserved for the most severe violations, demonstrating
  that the individual is unwilling or unable to participate in the community
  in a respectful manner.
-->

**Community Impact**: Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community
standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment of an
individual, or aggression toward or disparagement of classes of individuals.

**Consequence**: A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within
the community.
